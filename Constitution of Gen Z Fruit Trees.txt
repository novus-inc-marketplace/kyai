Constitution of Gen Z Fruit Trees


Preamble
We, the members of Gen Z Fruit Trees, united by a shared commitment to combat climate change, empower youth, and foster prosperity through the planting of high-value fruit trees, establish this Constitution to guide our organization. Rooted in action and dedicated to harvesting prosperity, we aim to create a sustainable future where nature and humanity thrive together.


Article I: Name and Mission
Name
The organization shall be known as "Gen Z Fruit Trees."
Mission
Gen Z Fruit Trees is a youth-led movement dedicated to planting high-value fruit trees-such as Hass avocado, macadamia, and mangoes-to fight climate change, create economic opportunities, ensure food security, and empower communities by 2030 and beyond.
Vision
By 2030, every household will have a fruit tree that feeds, empowers, and enriches, building a world where youth and nature prosper together.


Article II: Objectives
Environmental Restoration
To plant fruit trees that sequester carbon, cool urban areas, and restore ecosystems degraded by climate change.
Economic Empowerment
To create jobs, reduce poverty, and generate income for youth and communities through agroforestry and sustainable agriculture.
Education and Training
To provide free workshops on agroforestry, entrepreneurship, and carbon accounting, equipping members with skills for financial independence.
Community Engagement
To foster a global network of volunteers, advocates, and supporters committed to spreading the movement's mission.
Food Security
To increase access to fresh produce through widespread fruit tree planting, improving nutrition and resilience.


Article III: Membership
Eligibility
Membership is open to all individuals, regardless of age, who support the mission and vision of Gen Z Fruit Trees. Special emphasis is placed on engaging Generation Z (born 1997-2012) as leaders of the movement.
Categories of Membership  
Active Members: Individuals who volunteer, plant trees, or participate in workshops and advocacy efforts.  
Sponsors: Individuals or entities who adopt seedlings or provide financial support.  
Honorary Members: Recognized contributors to the movement, appointed by the Leadership Council.

Proposed Fee Categories
Basic Membership (Annual)  
Fee: $5 USD (or equivalent in local currency, e.g., KES 500 in Kenya)  
Eligibility: Open to all individuals, with a focus on youth (Gen Z).  
Benefits:  
Voting rights in elections and general meetings.  
Access to free agroforestry and entrepreneurship workshops.  
A digital membership certificate.  
Quarterly updates on tree-planting progress and impact.
Purpose: Ensures broad participation while covering administrative costs.

Grower Membership (Annual)  
Fee: $15 USD (or equivalent, e.g., KES 1,500)  

Eligibility: Individuals committed to actively planting and maintaining fruit trees.  




Benefits:  
All Basic Membership benefits.  
One free fruit tree seedling (e.g., Hass avocado or mango) per year.  
Priority access to nursery resources and planting drives.  
Recognition in annual reports as a "Green Warrior."
Purpose: Encourages hands-on participation and supports tree-planting goals.

Sponsor Membership (Annual)  
Fee: $50 USD (or equivalent, e.g., KES 5,000)  
Eligibility: Individuals, families, or small businesses wanting to make a larger impact.  
Benefits:  
All Grower Membership benefits.  
Sponsorship of 5 additional seedlings planted in underserved communities.  
A personalized "Tree Legacy" certificate with GPS coordinates of sponsored trees (when feasible).  
Invitation to exclusive annual events (e.g., harvest celebrations).

Purpose: Generates funds for scaling the movement and supporting community projects.

Lifetime Membership (One-Time)  
Fee: $150 USD (or equivalent, e.g., KES 15,000)  
Eligibility: Open to anyone seeking long-term commitment.  

Benefits:  
All Sponsor Membership benefits for life.  
A commemorative plaque or digital badge as a "Founding Planter."  
Permanent recognition on the Gen Z Fruit Trees website or honor roll.
Purpose: Builds a core group of dedicated supporters and provides upfront capital.



Volunteer Waiver (No Fee)  
Fee: Free  

Eligibility: Individuals who commit to volunteering at least 24 hours per year (e.g., 2 hours/month) in nurseries, planting drives, or advocacy.  
Benefits:  
Equivalent to Basic Membership benefits.  
Option to upgrade to Grower Membership at a 50% discount.
Purpose: Ensures inclusivity for those unable to pay while rewarding active participation.

Rights and Responsibilities  
Members have the right to participate in activities, vote in elections (if applicable), and access training programs.  
Members are responsible for upholding the mission, contributing time or resources, and promoting the organization.
Termination
Membership may be terminated by voluntary withdrawal or by a majority vote of the Leadership Council for actions contrary to the organization's values.


Article IV: Governance
Leadership Council  
The organization shall be governed by a Leadership Council elected by active members.  
The Council shall consist of:  
Chairperson: Oversees operations and represents the organization.  
Vice-Chairperson: Assists the Chairperson and acts in their absence.  
Treasurer: Manages finances and ensures transparency.  
Secretary: Records minutes, maintains records, and coordinates communication.  
Project Coordinator: Oversees tree-planting and training initiatives.  
Advocacy Lead: Drives outreach and awareness campaigns.

Terms of Office  
Council members serve two-year terms, renewable once by election.
Elections  
Elections shall occur annually or as needed, with voting open to all active members.
Decision-Making  
Decisions shall be made by a majority vote of the Leadership Council, with input from members encouraged during general meetings.



Article V: Operations
Programs  
Tree Adoption: Facilitate sponsorship of seedlings for planting by households and communities.  
Volunteer Initiatives: Organize planting drives, nursery management, and educational events.  
Training Workshops: Offer free skill-building sessions in agroforestry and entrepreneurship.  
Advocacy Campaigns: Promote the mission through social media, events, and partnerships.

Funding  
Funds shall be raised through donations, sponsorships, grants, and partnerships with carbon offset programs or businesses.  
All finances shall be tracked and reported publicly for transparency.

Accountability  
Annual reports shall detail trees planted, funds raised, and impacts achieved (e.g., jobs created, CO2 sequestered).  
An independent audit may be conducted if requested by a majority of members.


Article VI: Meetings
General Meetings  
Held at least twice a year to update members on progress, finances, and plans.
Leadership Council Meetings  
Held monthly or as needed to manage operations and strategy.
Special Meetings  
May be called by the Chairperson or by petition of at least 20% of active members.


Article VII: Amendments
Proposal  
Amendments to this Constitution may be proposed by any active member or the Leadership Council.
Approval  
Amendments require a two-thirds majority vote of active members present at a general meeting.
Notification  
Proposed amendments must be shared with members at least 14 days prior to voting.


Article VIII: Dissolution
Condition  
The organization may be dissolved by a two-thirds vote of active members if it can no longer fulfill its mission.
Assets  
Upon dissolution, remaining assets (e.g., funds, seedlings) shall be donated to a similar environmental or youth-focused organization.


Article IX: Core Values
Transparency: Openly sharing progress and financial details with the community.  
Accountability: Measuring and delivering on promised impacts.  
Empowerment: Equipping youth with tools for environmental and economic success.  
Sustainability: Prioritizing long-term ecological and social benefits.  
Collaboration: Building a movement through collective action.

Adoption
This Constitution is adopted on [Insert Date] by the founding members of Gen Z Fruit Trees and shall take effect immediately.
